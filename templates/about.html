About Performance Taekwon-Do

Performance Taekwon-Do was opened in February of 2014 in Halifax, Nova Scotia by Sean O'Neil. The club is currently located at St. Andrew's Community Center, 3380 Barnstead Lane, Halifax, Nova Scotia.

Performance Taekwon-Do focuses on developing elite martial artists with a particular emphasis on the sport aspect of ITF Taekwon-Do. The club also helps students develop a training regiment that keeps them in shape and healthy. Taekwon-Do, and martial arts in general, are a good way for students of any age to develop self esteem and a healthy lifestyle that will complement their lives outside of training. Martial arts are also used as a tool for social development - they teach self discipline and encourage their practitioners to be accountable for their progress and training. The instructors are extensively trained in multiple martial arts and therefore augment the typical Taekwon-Do cirriculum with the philosophy and teaching of different styles such as Karate and Kung-Fu.

 

Instructors

 

Sean O'Neil



Sean O'Neil is Performance Taekwon-Do's head instructor and a 4th degree black belt. Sean also holds a 2nd degree black belt in Chito-Ryu Karate from Ryuseikan in Dartmouth. Sean was born and raised in Halifax, Nova Scotia. In 2001, at the age of 6, Sean started training in Taekwon-Do at Tien-Lung Taekwon-Do in Halifax, and later trained at Mackenzie Taekwon-Do in Hammonds Plains. Sean has been actively training in Taekwon-Do since 2001. He has competed at many national and international level competitions. Sean has been the national champion in both patterns and sparring for many years, and has brought home many medals from the following World Cups and World Championships, including 4 gold. Sean also works as a software developer for a financial services company.

Wellington, New Zealand (2011)
Brighton, England (2012)
Benidorm, Spain (2013)
Montego Bay, Jamaica (2014)
Jesolo, Italy (2015)
Budapest, Hungary (2016)

Serena MacRury



Serena MacRury started training in Taekwon-Do in 2007 at the age of 9 at Hiltz Taekwon-Do in Lower Sackville. Serena joined Performance Taekwon-Do in 2016 as an assistant instructor. Serena is also a multiple time national champion in patterns and sparring and has represented Canada at the following World Championships. Serena also works as a veterinary technician at a local practice.
